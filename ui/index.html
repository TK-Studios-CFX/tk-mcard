You have the right to remain silent.
Anything you say can be used against you in a court of law.
You have the right to talk to an attorney.
You have the right to have an attorney with you during questioning.
If you cannot afford an attorney, one will be appointed for you before questioning if you wish.
If you decide to answer questions now without a lawyer present, you have the right to stop answering at any time.
